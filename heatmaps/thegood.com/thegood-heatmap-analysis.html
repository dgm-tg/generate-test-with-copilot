Methodology

This predictive heatmap was generated based on established UX principles, including:

  • F-pattern reading behavior typical for agency websites
  • Visual hierarchy principles
  • Digital agency website attention patterns based on industry research
  • Service showcase and portfolio best practices

The heatmap uses a color gradient from red (highest attention) to green (lowest attention).

User Attention Flow

The typical user attention flow on TheGood.com follows this pattern:

  1. Logo/branding recognition
  2. Main navigation scan
  3. Hero headline and primary CTA
  4. First service/feature offering
  5. Horizontal scanning of services
  6. First case study/portfolio item
  7. Additional case studies (with decreasing attention)
  8. Testimonial highlights
  9. About section heading (with less attention on details)
  10. Blog section brief scan
  11. Footer contact information

Optimization Recommendations

  1. Prioritize hero messaging - Ensure it clearly communicates value proposition
  2. Position key services on the left - Take advantage of the natural F-pattern
  3. Make primary CTA visually distinct - Use size, color and contrast to draw attention
  4. Feature strongest case study first - Users pay most attention to the first portfolio item
  5. Keep critical content above the fold - Attention decreases significantly as users scroll
